1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations. Investments in abatement and renewables follow.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy and 2030 goals.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across global operations by 2040.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, drastically lowering carbon emissions.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting leaders to collaborate on sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.
